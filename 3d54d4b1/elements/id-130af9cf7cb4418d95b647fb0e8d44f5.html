Regulation 910/2014 on electronic identification and trust services for electronic transactions ()
Description: Regulation 2014/910, also known as eIDAS (Electronic Identification, Authentication and Trust Services), is a European Union regulation that aims to establish a framework for electronic identification and trust services in the internal market. It provides a legal framework for electronic signatures, electronic seals, electronic time stamps, electronic documents, and electronic registered delivery services, ensuring their legal validity and cross-border recognition within the EU. The regulation aims to enhance the security and reliability of electronic transactions, promote the use of electronic identification, and facilitate the digital single market within the European Union.
